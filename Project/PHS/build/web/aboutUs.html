Paperless Hospital Service
TAPPI (Technical Association of the Pulp and Paper Industry) Report:
     17 trees are needed to make a ton of papers. A cord of wood is approximately 8 feet wide, 4 feet deep, and 4 feet high.
A cord of air-dried, dense hardwood (oak, hickory, etc.) weighs roughly 2 tons, about 15-20 percent of which is water.
      It has been estimated that one cord of this wood will yield one of these approximate quantities of products:
      1,000-2,000 pounds of paper (depending on the process) 942 100-page, hard-cover books 61,370 No. 10 business envelopes 2,700 copies of an average daily newspaper By studying and analyzing the TAPPI report, we calculated that a 100 bed Hospital which have 400 out patients uses approximately 8000 papers per day and on an average it consumes a tree per day.
"We can save one Tree per day by using PHS system"
     Objective of this project is to leverage paperless hospital service where hospitals need not use any paper-work while patient getting admitted to the hospital by providing seamless application that will handle the thousands of patients information and provide efficient healthcare service.
It is to align with SMART PLANET theme of IBM.


Proposed PHS System:
  • Patient can register online.
  • Patient can take appointment of doctor by just making a phone call or through an E-mail.
  • No paper usage for prescription, test reports, billing and Patient Case Sheet.
  • Patient can view their reports, bill, and other particulars details online.
  • It can deliver the final Patient Case Sheet as an E-mail or as a soft copy to the patient.
